## Business Context

**Academy** is an EdTech startup providing online learning content aimed at high school and university students. The management has requested an EDA to support the company's **international expansion strategy** — specifically to determine:

- Whether World Bank education datasets provide actionable insights for expansion decisions.
- Which countries show the strongest **potential demand** for online education.
- How that potential may **evolve over time**.

---

## Objectives

- Load and explore the World Bank EdStats dataset.
- Select and validate relevant education, expenditure, and connectivity indicators.
- Assess data quality (fill rates, missing values) across countries and years.
- Compute a composite **Education Potential Score** (0–100) per country per year.
- Identify the **top 20 target markets** for Academy's expansion.

## Analysis Pipeline

The analysis is split across three notebooks that run sequentially. Each notebook saves an intermediate file consumed by the next.

```
EdStatsSeries.csv ──┐
EdStatsData.csv  ───┤──► 01_data_loading ──► indicator_data.parquet
Indicators.xlsx  ───┘                        indicator_values.parquet
                                                      │
                                                      ▼
                                             02_processing ──► consolidated_table.parquet
                                                      │
                                                      ▼
                                          03_scoring_results ──► Top 20 countries
```

### Notebook 1 — Data Loading & Preprocessing (`01_data_loading.ipynb`)

1. **Load `EdStatsSeries.csv`** — 3,665 indicators × 21 columns; keep only `Series Code` and `Indicator Name`.
2. **Filter to selected indicators** — load `outputs/Indicators.xlsx` and retain only the 18 manually chosen Series Codes.
3. **Load `EdStatsData.csv`** — 886,930 rows covering all countries and years from 1970 to 2100.
4. **Country filtering** — remove 27 non-ISO aggregates (e.g. "Arab World", "Sub-Saharan Africa") using `pycountry`; retain 787,975 rows.
5. **Visualise missing values** — `missingno` matrix across year columns to understand data completeness.
6. **Year range filtering** — discard pre-2010 data (outdated) and post-2024 projections; add missing year columns as NaN. Retain 2010–2024 only.
7. **Save** `indicator_data.parquet` and `indicator_values.parquet` to `data/processed/`.

### Notebook 2 — Processing & Analysis (`02_processing.ipynb`)

1. **Load** parquet files from Notebook 1.
2. **Merge** `indicator_values` with `indicator_data` on `Indicator Code` (inner join) → 3,870 rows.
3. **Indicator selection** — filter to 8 pre-chosen indicators → 1,720 rows.
4. **Fill rate evaluation** — compute proportion of non-null values per indicator across 2010–2024; rank by average fill rate.
5. **Trend visualisation** — melt to long format, filter to 2010–2015, plot average evolution per indicator using seaborn line plots.
6. **Correlation analysis** — pivot to wide format, compute Pearson correlation matrix, visualise as heatmap.
7. **Flatten & enrich** — flatten the upper-triangle correlation matrix, join with indicator descriptions, sort by absolute correlation.
8. **Remove redundant indicators** — based on correlation results, drop 2 highly correlated indicators → 1,290 rows, 6 indicators remaining.
9. **Save** `consolidated_table.parquet` to `data/processed/`.

### Notebook 3 — Scoring & Results (`03_scoring_results.ipynb`)

1. **Load** `consolidated_table.parquet` from Notebook 2.
2. **Missing value imputation** — row-wise interpolation (forward + backward fill) per country-indicator pair; drop rows with no data at all. Visualise before/after with `missingno`.
3. **Reshape** — melt to long format, pivot so each indicator is a column; resulting `reshaped_table` has one row per country × year.
4. **Check remaining missing values** per indicator column.
5. **Compute Education Potential Score** (see [Education Potential Score](#education-potential-score) below).
6. **Drop rows** with no computable score → 1,725 rows retained.
7. **Identify top 20 countries** by score in the most recent scored year (2015).
8. **Visualise results**:
   - Bar chart: top 20 countries ranked by score
   - Line chart: score evolution over time for the top 20
   - Stacked area chart: cumulative score distribution over time

## Education Potential Score

A composite score (0–100) computed per country per year using two expenditure formulas averaged together, then normalised and weighted with internet access.

### Step 1 — Education Expenditure Calculation 1

```
Calc1 = Tertiary Enrolment × Government Expenditure per Tertiary Student (US$)
```

### Step 2 — Education Expenditure Calculation 2

```
Calc2 = GDP (current US$) × (Secondary Expenditure % GDP + Tertiary Expenditure % GDP) / 100
```

### Step 3 — Average

```
Average Education Expenditure = (Calc1 + Calc2) / 2
```
Only computed when both Calc1 and Calc2 are available; otherwise NaN.

### Step 4 — Normalisation

Both `Average Education Expenditure` and `Internet users (per 100 people)` are normalised to [0, 1] using **MinMaxScaler** fitted across all countries and years.

### Step 5 — Weighted Score

```
Score (raw) = 0.85 × Normalised Education Expenditure
            + 0.15 × Normalised Internet Users
```

### Step 6 — Scale to 0–100

```
Education Potential Score = Score (raw) × 100
```

Countries without sufficient data to compute both Calc1 and Calc2 are excluded from the ranking.

- The **United States** leads by a wide margin, driven by very high tertiary enrolment and government education expenditure in absolute terms.

